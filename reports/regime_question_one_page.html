When Does a Market Shock Become a Regime Problem?

A public-safe institutional evidence map from isolated market movement to regime-relevant transmission.
Answer upfront
A shock becomes regime-relevant when it spreads, persists, confirms across macro channels, and survives validation.
Research-desk interpretation
A large price move is not enough. The analytical question is whether the move remains isolated or becomes part of a broader transmission structure across rates, credit, FX, commodities, equities, volatility, and liquidity. The public artifact shows the logic without exposing private thresholds, weights, diagnostics, or action rules.
1

Shock origin

A move begins in one asset, channel, geography, or narrative.

2

Transmission

Stress spreads beyond the first market into correlated risk channels.

3

Persistence

The signal survives beyond a temporary volatility spike.

4

Confirmation

Macro, curve, credit, FX, and commodities begin to align.

5

Validation

The pattern survives leakage control, alternative windows, and robustness checks.

6

Regime risk

The shock becomes structurally relevant instead of merely noisy.

Why this question matters

1

2

3

4

5
